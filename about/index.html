Previously

I completed my B.Sc. in Cognitive Science at the University of Osnabrück. The study of Cognitive Science provided me with insights into the fields of computer science, mathematics, artificial intelligence, neuroinformatics, neuropsychology, computational linguistics and neurophilosophy. I graduated with a grade of 1.1 and honors.

I wrote my bachelor thesis in Bayesian Data Analysis, where I proposed a new statistical method to analyze a phenomenon called “Benford’s Law”. The $\chi^2$ test is an established method used to test data for compliance to Benford’s Law, but as I point out in my thesis, has several drawbacks compared to a Bayesian method.
